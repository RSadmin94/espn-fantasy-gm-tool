# Reveal Copy Examples — 15 Archetypes
**Atlantas Finest GM Tool · Sprint 1 · Pre-Code Copy Work**

Each example follows the same structure:
1. **Category label** (small caps, muted)
2. **Headline** (large, bold — the emotional punch)
3. **Evidence** (behavioral, specific, grounded)
4. **Implication** (brief, empowering — positions the user as ahead)

---

## Opponent Vulnerability Archetypes

### Example 1 — The Panic Seller (Emotional tone: urgency + opportunity)

**YOUR MOST EXPLOITABLE OPPONENT**

**Christian Is Vulnerable Right Now.**

Over the last 3 seasons, Christian's trade activity spikes after consecutive losses — and he consistently overpays in those windows.

*You have a small window before Week 4.*

---

### Example 2 — The Primetime Reactor (Emotional tone: insider knowledge)

**MOST LIKELY TO PANIC SELL**

**Snake Trades Emotionally After Primetime Games.**

3 of his last 4 trades were initiated within 48 hours of a Monday Night loss. He's your best trade partner right now — but only if you move first.

*Reach out before his next big game.*

---

### Example 3 — The Future-Pick Mark (Emotional tone: strategic superiority)

**MOST LIKELY TO ACCEPT YOUR OFFER**

**Nate Consistently Undervalues Future Picks.**

Over 4 seasons, Nate has accepted future 2nd-round picks at a rate 40% higher than the league average. He prioritizes now over later — every time.

*You have an edge here that the rest of your league hasn't found yet.*

---

### Example 4 — The Overconfident Contender (Emotional tone: rivalry + threat)

**BIGGEST THREAT TO YOUR TITLE**

**Christian Is Playing His Best Fantasy Football Right Now.**

12–2 last season. Elite roster construction. And he's already locked his keeper. The window to trade with him before he becomes untouchable is closing.

*He won't stay this accessible for long.*

---

### Example 5 — The Frustrated Rebuilder (Emotional tone: timing + leverage)

**BEST TRADE PARTNER RIGHT NOW**

**Mark Is Ready To Deal.**

3–11 last season. He's been in rebuild mode for 18 months. Managers in this position historically accept present-value offers at a significant discount.

*He wants wins now. You can give him that — at the right price.*

## Self-Insight Archetypes

### Example 6 — The Draft Weakness (Emotional tone: slightly uncomfortable, trust-building)

**YOUR BIGGEST DRAFT WEAKNESS**

**You Consistently Reach For Aging RBs.**

In rounds 3–5, your draft history shows a pattern: you overvalue veteran running backs after strong playoff finishes. The league has started to anticipate this.

*Your 2026 draft edge is in rounds 6–9, where you've historically outperformed.*

---

### Example 7 — The Trade Blind Spot (Emotional tone: self-awareness, empowerment)

**YOUR TRADE BLIND SPOT**

**You Rarely Initiate Trades — And It's Costing You.**

Over 7 seasons, you've initiated fewer trades than 11 of 14 managers in your league. The managers who've won championships here have all been aggressive buyers before Week 6.

*This is your most actionable edge right now.*

---

### Example 8 — The Keeper Strength (Emotional tone: confidence, identity)

**YOUR BIGGEST COMPETITIVE EDGE**

**You Draft Better Than Anyone In This League.**

Over 8 seasons, your draft picks outperform their ADP at a higher rate than any other manager. This is your most repeatable advantage — and most of your league doesn't know it yet.

*Protect it. Don't trade away picks you don't have to.*

---

### Example 9 — The Waiver Weakness (Emotional tone: honest, grounding)

**WHERE YOU LOSE GROUND**

**You're Slow On The Waiver Wire.**

Historically, you add players 2–3 days later than the top managers in your league. In a 14-team league with this much waiver competition, that gap compounds over a season.

*The managers who've beaten you consistently are faster here.*

## League-Wide Insight Archetypes

### Example 10 — The Market Inefficiency (Emotional tone: strategic, exclusive)

**LEAGUE MARKET INEFFICIENCY**

**Future 2nd-Round Picks Are Undervalued In Your League.**

After Week 5, managers in Atlantas Finest historically discount future 2nd-round picks by an average of 30% relative to dynasty value charts. This pattern has held for 4 consecutive seasons.

*This is your most repeatable trade edge. Use it before the league adjusts.*

---

### Example 11 — The Championship Pattern (Emotional tone: identity, aspiration)

**HOW CHAMPIONSHIPS ARE WON HERE**

**Every Title In This League Was Built On Draft Capital.**

In 8 seasons, every champion entered the playoffs with at least 2 picks in the top 3 rounds. No manager has won this league by trading away future picks — ever.

*Your current pick inventory puts you in that conversation.*

---

### Example 12 — The Rivalry Insight (Emotional tone: social, charged)

**YOUR DEFINING RIVALRY**

**You And Christian Have Split The Last 6 Matchups Evenly.**

3–3 head-to-head over 3 seasons. The deciding factor in every matchup: RB depth in weeks 12–14. Whoever has more healthy RBs in that window wins.

*That's the specific edge to build toward.*

## Timing and Urgency Archetypes

### Example 13 — The Keeper Window (Emotional tone: urgency, loss aversion)

**TIME-SENSITIVE INTELLIGENCE**

**Your Keeper Window Is Closing.**

The managers who've won this league have locked their keepers early — before the market adjusts. Two managers have already signaled their keeper decisions. Once they're locked, the draft board shifts.

*You have 48 days. The first 14 are the most important.*

---

### Example 14 — The Offseason Positioning (Emotional tone: strategic calm)

**YOUR 2026 POSITION**

**You're Better Positioned Than Your Record Suggests.**

9–5 last season, #1 seed, champion. But your underlying metrics — points differential, roster depth, draft capital — rank you 2nd in the league for 2026 title probability.

*The league underestimates you. That's an advantage.*

---

### Example 15 — The Behavioral Tell (Emotional tone: intelligence, slight menace)

**INTELLIGENCE REPORT**

**Demetri Negotiates Differently Than He Trades.**

In 4 seasons of transaction data, Demetri has initiated trades he later regretted at a higher rate than any other manager in this league. He's aggressive in negotiation but reactive in execution.

*He'll make you a big offer. Don't accept the first one.*

## Selection Notes

**The 3 strongest for the live Reveal (recommendation):**

| Slot | Example | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Main Reveal | Example 1 — Christian Is Vulnerable Right Now | Urgent, personal, behavioral, time-pressured. Passes the "surprisingly plausible" test. |
| Self-Insight | Example 6 — You Consistently Reach For Aging RBs | Slightly uncomfortable, specific, trust-building. The moment users think "wait, that's actually true." |
| League Insight | Example 10 — Future 2nd-Round Picks Are Undervalued | Strategic, exclusive, actionable. Positions the user as ahead of the league. |

**Why these three together:** The main reveal is about an opponent (external threat). The self-insight is about the user (internal honesty). The league insight is about the market (strategic edge). Together they cover all three emotional registers: threat, self-awareness, and opportunity. That combination is what makes the reveal feel comprehensive rather than cherry-picked.

**The rivalry card (Example 2 — Snake) and the market inefficiency (Example 10) are strong alternates** if the primary selections feel too similar in tone.

## Revised Suspense Layer Copy (4.5–5.5 seconds, skip after line 2)

```
Analyzing 9 seasons of league history…        [0–1.2s]

Mapping rivalry patterns across 126 matchups… [1.2–2.5s]  ← skip available after this line

Detecting trade behavior and panic signals…   [2.5–3.8s]

Identifying your most exploitable opponent…   [3.8–5.0s]
```

Skip behavior: after line 2, a subtle "Skip →" appears in the bottom-right corner in muted text. It does not flash or animate. Users who want to skip can. Users who don't will never notice it.

## Revised Implication Line (incorporating "you have a small window")

**Old:** *Strike before Week 4. He'll be motivated to move.*

**New:** *You have a small window before Week 4. He'll be motivated to deal.*

The shift from "Strike" (imperative, aggressive) to "You have a small window" (empowering, personal) is the key change. It positions the user as informed and ahead — not just instructed.
